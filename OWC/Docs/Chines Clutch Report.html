Motor Current Analysis

Forward Direction Analysis

Phase Current (2.29A): Indicates normal motor operation during forward rotation.

DC Current (0.8A): Represents the steady-state power consumption for maintaining rotation at 320 RPM.

Reverse Direction Analysis

Initial Spike (1.5A DC): Brief current spike during attempted reverse rotation, indicating proper clutch locking mechanism engagement.

Steady State (0.8A DC): Current drops to normal level once clutch lock is confirmed and motor stops attempting rotation.

Zero Phase Current: Confirms complete prevention of reverse rotation by the one-way clutch mechanism.

Electrical System Health
Current consumption patterns remained consistent throughout the test period, indicating stable electrical performance. The power supply interruptions affecting the Raspberry Pi 5 did not impact the motor controller or clutch electrical characteristics.
